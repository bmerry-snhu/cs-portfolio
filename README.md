# Reflection

## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

Understanding user needs begins with listening to stakeholders rather than making assumptions about what users want. Throughout this course, I learned that interviews, feedback sessions, and ongoing communication provide valuable insight into customer priorities. User stories transform those conversations into clear development goals by describing functionality from the user's perspective. This helps the development team focus on delivering value instead of simply implementing technical features. User stories also provide a shared understanding between developers, testers, Product Owners, and stakeholders, making it easier to prioritize work and verify that completed features satisfy customer expectations.

## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach software development as an iterative process that emphasizes planning, communication, and continuous improvement. Rather than attempting to define every requirement before development begins, Agile encourages teams to deliver software in small increments while adapting to changing priorities through customer feedback. This course reinforced the importance of Sprint Planning, backlog refinement, Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Sprint Retrospectives as practical methods for maintaining alignment throughout development. In future projects, I hope to continue using these Agile practices because they reduce uncertainty, improve collaboration, and allow software to evolve as requirements change.

## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating openly, collaborating with others, and taking shared responsibility for delivering value to users. Every Scrum role depends on timely communication to clarify requirements, identify blockers, and adapt to changing priorities. This course demonstrated that successful software development is not the result of individual effort alone but of effective collaboration among developers, testers, Product Owners, Scrum Masters, stakeholders, and customers. A strong team member contributes technical work while also helping create an environment where information is shared openly, feedback is welcomed, and continuous improvement becomes part of the team's culture.
